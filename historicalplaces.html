ST.PIERRE CHURCH

St. Pierre Church is located on the west side of Asi River and by the west foot of Hac (Staurin) Mountain. The area where the church was constructed is the antique city of Antiokheia. As the Antiokheia got bigger between II. and IV. Centuries, church turned to be in the middle of the city, near the Agora. This means the building was important in the social life of people in that era. Although there is no concrete data on the exact construction date, the oral tradition, historical events and development process of the city suggest that the building was used since the first Christians in Antakya.

The west facade was constructed by Kapuchin Priests in 19th century. The traces on the facade and platform in front suggest that there was a portico here formerly. West facade show a regional style which has a threefold layout. This facade was constructed by hewn stones and has three portals, middle one of which is bigger than the others. The platform of hewn stones, altar and St. Pierre sculpture in a niche belong to recent times.


ARCHAEOLOGY MUSEUM

The museum in `Hatay` Antakya is regarded as the second richest mosaic museum in the world in terms of the number and quality of the mosaics exhibited.

The mythological symbols on the mosaics, the excavations made in 1932, came to light again. The mosaics of the baths, houses and churches bearing the traces of the Roman and Byzantine eras continue to exist in Samandagi, Antakya and Harbiye.

There are also statues in the museum. The god of the Sun God Apollon is the most important among these. These rare artifacts are exhibited at the Antique Mosaic Museum. The mythological subjects covered in mosaics will meet you with all the Olympus gods.

St.Simon Monastery, known as the center of the Terk-i World sect, is located between Samandað and Antakya and is 18 km away from Antakya. It is located near the village of Nahirli and built on a hill overlooking the bed of the Asi River. The name of the mountain is taken from St.Simon, who takes religious education at very young ages and devotes himself entirely to God. After this heavy religious education, St. Simon wanted to get away from the people and wanted to be close to the god alone. It has begun to live on the column in the middle of the monastery which reaches to the sunrise.

The name has started to be heard by everyone from day to day and the illness has begun to heal. Simon, who began to suffer from the Christian visitor movement, was exalted by people to go. He lived on this column for 40 years. The mountain where he was found to be healing people was called the Miracle Mountain.
